Smarttie — Brand Identity & Guidelines (2020)

A pragmatic rebrand for a software development agency: a modern logotype and symbol system, documented with clear usage rules, grid guidance, and ready-to-apply templates for print and digital.

Category Brand Identity · Visual System
Role Brand & UI Designer
Year 2020

Context

Smarttie is a software development agency positioned at the intersection of product craftsmanship and high-tech execution. In 2020, the team needed a brand identity that could scale across client-facing touchpoints (website, proposals, decks) while staying simple enough to be implemented consistently by a fast-moving delivery organization.

My goal was not to create “branding for branding’s sake,” but to build a usable visual system: one that codifies decisions, reduces ambiguity, and makes quality repeatable.

Goals

  • Create a modern identity that feels confident, friendly, and technology-forward.
  • Establish clear, enforceable usage rules so the brand remains consistent across print and digital.
  • Provide implementation-ready assets and guidance (logo usage, typography, grid, applications).
  • Reduce visual drift by documenting decisions in a practical brandbook teams can actually follow.

Strategy

The design approach centered on clarity and recognizability. The logotype uses a bold, highly legible wordmark designed to stand up across contexts—from small favicons to large-format print—while the supporting symbol extends the identity into a flexible system.

A key choice was to keep the system intentionally constrained: limited logo colors, documented spacing rules, and predictable layouts. Constraints are what make a brand scalable.

Core Identity

The identity system includes two primary components: the logotype and a supporting symbol. Together, they create a cohesive brand signature and a recognizable visual anchor across assets.

  • Logotype: designed for immediate readability and strong presence in both black/white and brand color.
  • Symbol: a graphic extension that provides flexibility for patterns, layouts, and branded moments without relying on illustration-heavy assets.

Logo Governance

A brand is only as strong as its consistency. To prevent accidental distortion and misuse, the brandbook includes practical governance: approved colorways, minimum sizes, clear space, and “do not” rules.

Proper Usage Across Backgrounds

Real-world usage introduces complexity: backgrounds change, contrast shifts, and layouts vary. The guidelines define which logo version to use by surface—light, dark, and photographic backgrounds—so the identity stays legible and consistent.

Typography & Print Applications

Typography decisions were documented for consistent hierarchy, spacing, and tone. Print applications (business cards, letterhead, envelope) were designed as templates—not as one-off visuals—so the system could scale with minimal ongoing design effort.

Grid System for Digital Applications

To make the brand usable beyond print, the guidelines include a digital grid framework. This makes layout decisions more predictable, supports responsive behavior, and helps keep marketing pages and product surfaces aligned to a single visual logic.

Website Application

The brand system was applied to a website concept that demonstrates how the identity scales in a digital environment—hero composition, navigation styling, brand color usage, and typographic hierarchy working together as a coherent whole.

Results

  • A cohesive identity system that balances personality with implementation constraints.
  • A usable brandbook with rules that reduce inconsistency and simplify decision-making.
  • Production-ready assets and templates for print and digital touchpoints.
  • A scalable foundation that supports future growth without re-inventing the brand every time a new asset is needed.

What I’d Do Next (If Continuing)

If this engagement continued, the next step would be to translate the guidelines into an operational system: tokenized color and type rules for the website, a lightweight component library for common marketing blocks, and a QA checklist that makes brand consistency measurable at release time.
