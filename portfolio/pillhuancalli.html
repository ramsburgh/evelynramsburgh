Pillhuancalli Montessori — Non-profit Educational Website

Pillhuancalli Montessori is a non-profit educational institution supporting children, single mothers, and individuals with very limited financial resources. I led the frontend and UI design of their website to translate the mission into a clear, accessible digital experience aligned with the UN Sustainable Development Goals.

Category Non-profit · Education · Social Impact
Role Frontend / UI Designer
Focus Accessibility · SDGs · Information clarity

About

In 2022, I led the frontend and UI design efforts for the Pillhuancalli Montessori website. The organization works with underserved communities, providing education and support programs for children, single mothers, and individuals living in vulnerable conditions.

The website needed to balance emotional storytelling with practical clarity: communicate the institution’s values and programs while remaining accessible and easy to navigate for a broad and diverse audience.

Objective

Design a user-friendly and inclusive website that clearly communicates Pillhuancalli Montessori’s mission, highlights alignment with relevant UN Sustainable Development Goals, and encourages community engagement.

Goals

  • Translate the organization’s mission into a clear, approachable digital experience.
  • Highlight meaningful alignment with specific UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
  • Design an accessible interface for users with different levels of digital literacy.
  • Strengthen trust and engagement through clarity, hierarchy, and visual consistency.

Process

The project followed a research-driven approach focused on communication clarity, accessibility, and purpose-led visual decisions—ensuring the interface supports the mission rather than competing with it.

Research & Discovery

  • Reviewed programs, audience needs, and institutional messaging constraints.
  • Mapped initiatives to relevant SDGs to decide what to highlight and where.
  • Identified the key moments where users need clarity: mission, programs, impact, and how to support.

Information Architecture & Wireframing

I structured the site to guide users through a simple narrative: who the organization is, what they do, who they support, and how the community can get involved—reducing cognitive load and improving discoverability.

  • Clear separation between mission, programs, and community impact.
  • Simple navigation patterns to support scanning and quick comprehension.
  • Content hierarchy designed for readability across devices.

Visual Design & UI

The UI prioritizes warmth, trust, and approachability while keeping the layout highly readable. Accessibility choices (contrast, type scale, spacing) were treated as part of the product quality—not an afterthought.

  • Readable typography and spacing for long-form content.
  • Accessible contrast and consistent UI patterns.
  • Visual hierarchy designed to support comprehension and decision-making.

Frontend Implementation

I delivered frontend-ready UI and worked closely with implementation to preserve spacing, hierarchy, and accessibility. The goal was to ensure the final experience matches the intended reading flow and remains usable across devices.

Final Remarks

This project shows how UX/UI can support social impact by making information easy to understand, inclusive, and emotionally respectful. Aligning the experience to the SDGs helped frame the organization’s initiatives in a recognizable global context—while keeping the site grounded in real community needs.

The website was in development at the time of delivery and planned for launch in early 2022, providing Pillhuancalli Montessori with a digital presence that reflects its mission and improves outreach.
